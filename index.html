A bit about me
The short version

I'm a product designer who helps teams turn complex problems into digital experiences that are clear, usable, and genuinely enjoyable. I care about the details that make someone's day 1% easier and I obsess over the structure that makes products scale.

My path into design wasn't traditional. I started in visual design and branding back in 2015, working on logos, identity systems, and print collateral. That foundation taught me typography, color theory, and most importantly, how to listen to what a client actually needs vs. what they initially ask for.

Around 2020, I made the jump to UI/UX and haven't looked back. The transition from "making things look good" to "making things work well" fundamentally changed how I approach every project. Today I focus on end-to-end product design research, wireframing, visual design, prototyping, design systems, and user testing.

"I'm most energized by complex, data-heavy products where information architecture matters as much as aesthetics enterprise tools, health tech, and platforms that serve real workflows."

When I'm not designing, I'm exploring new tools, dissecting case study breakdowns, or overthinking button border-radius values. I take my craft seriously, but not myself.
