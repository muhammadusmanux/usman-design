Design Process

From Insight to Impact

A structured approach to building a learning experience that adapts to each learner's journey treated like a curriculum itself.

STEP 01

  • Created 3 key personas: Career Switchers, Skill Upgraders, Continuous Learners

Problem & Solution

Bridging the Skills Gap

Addressing the core challenges that prevent professionals from continuous growth.

The Challenge
Working professionals want to upskill but struggle with finding relevant content, staying motivated, and proving their learning to employers.
All while balancing full-time jobs, personal commitments, and the overwhelming paradox of choice across dozens of learning platforms. Platform drop-off data and forum research consistently point to the same root cause: the #1 reason isn't content quality — it's time fragmentation and zero accountability.
Self-Imposed Constraints
• 10-week timeline, solo designer · • WCAG AA accessibility (minimum 4.5:1 contrast) · • 8px base grid system · • Responsive web + native iOS patterns · • Micro-lessons must fit in 15–30 minute sessions · • Must work offline for commute scenarios · • Public research data only no proprietary learning datasets

Testing Methodology
I evaluated the prototype against Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics and walked through 5 core tasks: (1) Find and start a recommended course, (2) Resume an in-progress lesson from the dashboard, (3) Take a timestamped note during a lesson, (4) Check weekly progress and identify weakest skill, (5) Switch from desktop to mobile and continue watching. The "40% friction reduction" is a projected estimate comparing Stride's task flow depth against published benchmarks for Coursera and Udemy.
Reflections & Trade-offs

What I'd Do Differently

Honest trade-offs and the changes I'd make with more time, scope, or resources.

REFLECTION 01
Streak Mechanics Need Nuance
The current streak system rewards daily activity but a rigid streak can feel punishing when life gets in the way. With more time: I'd design a "flex day" system (2 penalty-free skip days per week) and test whether it improves long-term retention without reducing engagement.
REFLECTION 02
Onboarding Was Cut for Time
The current design drops users into the dashboard with pre-populated recommendations. But without an onboarding quiz, the AI recommendations have no initial data. With more time: I'd build a 3-step onboarding flow collecting career goals, time budget, and current skill level the "Recommended for You" section means nothing without it.
REFLECTION 03
Community ≠ Comments Section
I designed forums and Q&A, but real community needs more: study groups, cohort-based learning, accountability partners. The current feature set is closer to "comments under a video" than genuine peer support. The lesson: Community features need their own design sprint bolting them onto a content platform produces shallow engagement.
REFLECTION 04
The Lesson Player Was the Hardest Screen
Getting video + notes + curriculum to coexist on mobile was the biggest design challenge. V1 tried tabs below the video, V2 used a split-screen gesture both failed in testing. The final tab-based approach works, but it's a compromise. What I'd add: A "focus mode" that hides everything except video + one active note card, triggered by a single tap.
