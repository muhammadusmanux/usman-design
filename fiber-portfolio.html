Problem & Solution

Five Features. One Mission.

Every feature in Fiber maps directly to a specific user frustration uncovered in research not a wish list, a response.

Restaurant Finder
Surfaces diabetic-safe dining spots nearby with menu filters and peer-reviewed safety ratings from the Fiber community.
Smart Food Assistant
AI-powered meal scanner analyses food in real time indicating whether it's diabetes-safe with a plain-language explanation.
Core Problem
Managing diabetes around food is full of uncertainty
Users can't quickly identify safe meals, don't have a reliable way to track glucose trends, and lack a community that understands their challenges.
5 targeted features
BGL Monitor
Simple blood glucose logging with visual trend charts helping users spot patterns without needing expensive CGM devices.
Community Hub
A safe space to share dining experiences, restaurant reviews, and glucose insights with others navigating the same challenges.
Medical ID Emergency Readiness
Stores critical health data (medications, allergies, emergency contacts) accessible from the lock screen in seconds.
Self-Imposed Constraints
• 8-week timeline, solo designer · • WCAG AA accessibility (min 4.5:1 contrast) · • iOS HIG compliance · • 8px base grid · • Color-blind safe text labels + shape cues (✓ / ◐ / ⚠) for all status indicators · • Medical data must be scannable under stress · • Offline access for BGL readings · • No proprietary health datasets

Testing Methodology
Moderated usability testing with 12 participants (ages 22–58, all living with Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes) using a clickable Figma prototype. Each session was 40 minutes. Participants attempted 5 core tasks: (1) Scan a meal and check if it's safe, (2) Log a blood glucose reading, (3) Find a nearby diabetic-friendly restaurant, (4) Access their Medical ID from the lock screen, (5) Post a question in the community. Task completion was binary (completed within 3 min). The "< 8s scan time" was the median across all 12 participants on task 1. Satisfaction used a 1–5 Likert scale across 6 questions.
Reflections & Trade-offs

What I'd Do Differently

Honest trade-offs and changes I'd make with more time, scope, or medical expertise.

REFLECTION 01
Medical Accuracy Needs Professional Review
The food scanning feature flags meals as "safe" or "caution" based on carb content but diabetes management is far more nuanced (insulin ratios, time of day, exercise). With more time: I'd partner with an endocrinologist to validate the safety classification logic and add personalized thresholds.
REFLECTION 02
Emergency UX Needs Real-World Testing
The Medical ID screen was designed for paramedics but I tested it with designers, not actual EMTs. The information hierarchy might be wrong under real emergency pressure. With more time: I'd shadow EMTs and test the screen in simulated triage scenarios.
REFLECTION 03
Community Moderation Is Unsolved
The community feed lets users share meals and tips but health misinformation is dangerous. Who flags bad advice? How do you verify "safe" recipes? The lesson: Medical community features need expert moderation by design, not as an afterthought. I'd add verified dietitian badges and a peer-review system.
REFLECTION 04
Color-Blind Accessibility Was Hard
Using green/amber/red for safe/caution/danger is intuitive but fails for red-green color blindness (8% of males). I added text labels and shape cues (✓ / ◐ / ⚠) as secondary indicators, but the visual hierarchy still leans on color. What I'd add: A full color-blind mode toggle with blue/orange/purple ramp.
